Ethiopia

ETHIOPIAN COFFEE

Some of the most sought-after cafes in the world are produced in Ethiopia. Some coffees produced there are naturally low in caffeine.


Kenya

KENYAN COFFEE

Kenyan coffee is known for its biting, fruity, lemon or citrus taste, due to its high acidity.

Uganda

UGANDAN COFFEE

Uganda produces mainly Robusta and an excellent washed Arabica (grown largely in the Bugisu region).


DRC

DRC COFFEE

Most of the arabicas harvested in the Congo have a beautiful bright blue/green blue color of homogeneous appearance. The best Arabica coffees in the Kivu Mountains are described as fine, perfectly balanced coffees with body and acidity.

Tanzania

TANZANIAN COFFEE

Tanzania is the producer of an excellent washed Arabica reminiscent of Kenyan coffee, even though its acidity is less intense and the overall impression is that of a softer and lighter coffee.


Angola

ANGOLAN COFFEE

Although Robusta coffee is generally considered to be a less-than-good coffee, the one in Angola (Ambriz and Amboin) has a superior quality to third parties. The beans are uniform in size and color. The Robusta of Angola is grown on shady coffee fields of the northern Plains, near the Congo Delta.

Madagascar

MADAGASCAN COFFEE

Madagascar is a producer of Robusta (mainly harvested from the eastern seaboard) and Arabica (central Plateau). The Malagasy coffee is largely exported to France. Overall, the coffee grown in Madagascar is of good quality.


Cameroon

CAMEROONIAN COFFEE

Although one third of its harvest is a Blue Mountains variety Arabica, Cameroon is a very large producer of Robusta. Their production is mainly wet-treated.


Ghana

GHANAIAN COFFEE

In regions not adapted to cocoa cultivation, Ghana harvests regular Robusta which is essentially purchased by England, Germany and the Netherlands.


Cote d'Ivoire

IVORIAN COFFEE

In Côte d’ivoire a medium-quality Robusta is harvested which, due to the constancy and reliability of its agricultural culture, makes it attractive to the manufacturers of mixtures. It is mainly exported to France and Italy.
